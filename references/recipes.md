# The Recipe Library

This is what makes the skill more than a generic planner: a real rotation of dinners worth
cooking, written the way they actually get made. When planning a week, **pull dinners from
here first**, then suggest new dishes in the same spirit (bold flavor, one pan where possible,
weeknight-fast).

**Two rules for this file:**
1. **Only original recipes and personal adaptations go here in full.** This file ships in a

2. **Cookbook-inspired dishes get attributed pointers, not copies** — the technique in your
   own words, credit to the source, and a nudge to buy the book. See the examples below.

## House rotation

### Rotation Bowl (default lunch / leftover destination)
The workhorse. Infinitely swappable, always satisfying, great for using up cooked protein.
- **Formula:** protein + ~1 cup cooked grain + 1.5–2 cups hot veg + 2 cups raw volume +
  1–1.5 tbsp fat + bold seasoning.
- **Always:** 2 tbsp balsamic + 1 tbsp Worcestershire (~40 cal / 8g net carb) — the move that
  makes it taste like something.
- **Proteins:** chicken, pork chop, ground turkey, salmon, shrimp. **Grains:** farro (weigh
  dry — 45g ≈ 170 cal), brown rice, quinoa, lentils. **Raw volume:** arugula, endive, cucumber.
- **Why it works:** big volume, high protein, one bowl, zero-decision when you've got leftovers.

### Turkey burgers
Weeknight default, kid-friendly, scales to a crowd.
- Griddle the patties hard for crust; potato buns.
- **Spread:** hummus instead of mayo — lighter, still rich.
- **On top:** avocado + heirloom tomato. **Sides:** raw snap peas, cucumbers for the kids.
- Keep any heat off the patty and offer hot sauce at the table.
- **Leftover move:** crumble a spare patty into a Rotation Bowl the next day.

### Shrimp tacos
Fast, feels like a treat, good "earned the carbs" dinner after a hard training day.
- Mini tortillas, taco-seasoned shrimp seared hot and quick.
- Quick cucumber-tomato salsa with lime (lemon works in a pinch).
- Avocado. Thaw frozen shrimp in the fridge the morning of.

### Seared pork chops + melted fennel & lemon
*My take on the Caroline Chambers school of one-pan dinners — for her actual recipes, get*
*What to Cook When You Don't Feel Like Cooking.*
- Sear chops hard, rest under foil.
- In the same pan, soften sliced fennel in the drippings with garlic + a splash of water;
  finish with lemon juice/zest and shaved parmesan.
- Jasmine rice + roasted broccoli for the table.
- **Leftover move:** cold sliced pork is a top-tier Rotation Bowl protein.

### Crispy gnocchi + charred squash + feta
*Inspired by the Molly Baz crisp-don't-boil approach — her version's in Cook This Book.*
- Crisp the gnocchi in a hot dry pan; no boiling.
- Char zucchini + yellow squash alongside; toss warm with lemon, herbs, feta over endive.
- **Protein flag:** this one runs light — fry 1–2 eggs per adult on top to make it respectable.
- Doesn't hold as leftovers; cook what you'll eat.

## Flavor principles (the house style)
Apply these to anything, new recipe or old:
- **Bold seasoning, always:** hot sauce, smoked paprika, cumin, garlic, fresh herbs, citrus,
  vinegar. A "healthy" plate that tastes flat won't get cooked twice.
- **Texture in every meal:** something crunchy + something soft/chewy.
- **One pan / cast iron** whenever it'll work — fewer dishes, better sear.
- **Volume veg on every plate:** arugula, broccoli, spinach, cucumber, peppers.
- **Kids eat the same food** — heat and strong spice go on at the table, not in the batch.
