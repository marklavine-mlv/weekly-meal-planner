# Satiety, Adherence & Leftovers

The reusable principles behind a plan people actually cook and stick to. Apply these
regardless of anyone's specific diet.

## Adherence beats variety
- **Build around a small rotation of reliable meals**, not a novel dish every night. People
  stick to plans made of things they already like and know how to cook.
- **Reuse the user's staples** before introducing anything new. A plan that empties the
  fridge and pantry is a good plan.
- **Match effort to the day.** Simple, one-pan, fast on busy nights; save the involved dish
  for a day with time.

## Satiety (makes a plan filling without blowing the budget)
- **Add high-volume, low-calorie vegetables to every meal** — leafy greens, broccoli,
  cucumber, peppers, snap peas. Volume drives fullness.
- **Vary texture within a meal** — something crunchy + something soft/chewy eats more
  satisfying than a uniform plate.
- **Season boldly** — herbs, citrus, garlic, spice, vinegar. Flavor is what makes a "diet"
  meal feel like a real meal.
- **Prioritize high-fiber carbs** (whole grains, legumes, high-fiber breads/cereals) over
  refined ones — steadier fullness.
- **Protein at every meal.** Anchor each plate on a protein source.

## Leftovers as a feature, not an afterthought
- **Estimate leftovers for each cook night** (roughly how many portions remain).
- **Route every leftover to a job**: next-day lunch, a second dinner, or the freezer. Say
  where it goes in the plan.
- **Bulk-cook deliberately** when it creates good leftovers — e.g. cooking a whole protein
  pack at once to bank zero-decision lunches.
- **Note what doesn't keep** (e.g. gnocchi, dressed salads) and plan to finish it the night
  it's made.
- **Close the loop:** the end-of-plan leftover summary should tell the user exactly what
  they'll be eating on the flex days, so those days need little or no extra shopping.

## Family/table dynamics
- **One meal for the table.** Avoid cooking separate kids' meals unless asked.
- **Keep heat/spice out of the shared batch** and offer it at the table, so the same dish
  works for adults and kids.
- **Scale up, portion down:** cook family-scale quantities, then define the individual plate
  (and macros) for whoever is tracking.
